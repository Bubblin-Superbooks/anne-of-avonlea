Miss Lavendar looked at her tea table again, and blushed.

“I know you’ll think me dreadfully foolish,” she said. “I AM foolish . . . and I’m ashamed of it when I’m found out, but never unless I AM found out. I’m not expecting anybody . . . I was just pretending I was. You see, I was so lonely. I love company . . . that is, the right kind of company.. .but so few people ever come here because it is so far out of the way. Charlotta the Fourth was lonely too. So I just pretended I was going to have a tea party. I cooked for it . . . and decorated the table for it.. . and set it with my mother’s wedding china . . . and I dressed up for it.” Diana secretly thought Miss Lavendar quite as peculiar as report had pictured her.
